Chelsea

History of Chelsea Football Club

Chelsea FC, founded in 1905, is based in Fulham, London. The club was established at Stamford Bridge, a ground originally intended for athletics but transformed into a football stadium.
Chelsea quickly joined the Football League and gained a reputation for their large crowds and big-name signings, though early success was limited.
Chelsea won their first major trophy, the League Championship, in 1955. However, consistent success eluded the club until the 1990s, when new ownership and investments revitalized the team.
Roman Abramovich's takeover in 2003 marked a turning point, enabling Chelsea to become one of England’s most successful clubs.

Achievements

Chelsea has a rich history of trophies, including:
  • English League Titles: 6 (1955, 2005, 2006, 2010, 2015, 2017)
  • FA Cups: 8 (first in 1970, most recent in 2018)
  • League Cups: 5
  • UEFA Champions League: 2 (2012, 2021)
  • UEFA Europa League: 2 (2013, 2019)
  • UEFA Super Cup: 2
  • FIFA Club World Cup: 1 (2021)

Chelsea is one of the few clubs to win every major UEFA competition.



Legends of Chelsea

Gianfranco Zola: Known for his skill, creativity, and sportsmanship, Zola is regarded as one of Chelsea’s all-time greats.
Frank Lampard: The club’s all-time leading scorer, Lampard was central to Chelsea’s success during the 2000s and early 2010s.
